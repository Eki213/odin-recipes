Cheesecake

Description

Cheesecake is a rich, creamy dessert made from a smooth mixture of cream cheese, eggs, and sugar, typically set on a crust of crushed cookies or graham crackers. It can be either baked or no-bake, with variations ranging from the classic New York-style to fruit-topped, chocolate, or unique flavors like matcha and caramel.

Loved for its velvety texture and perfect balance of sweetness and tanginess, cheesecake remains a popular dessert worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 ¾ cups Honey Maid graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup butter, melted
  • 1 ¼ cups sugar, divided
  • 3 (8-ounce) packages Philadelphia cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup Breakstone's or Knudsen sour cream
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 (21-ounce) can cherry pie filling

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. Prepare the crust: In a large bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and ¼ cup sugar until well combined. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan.
  3. Make the filling: In a separate large bowl, beat the cream cheese and the remaining sugar with a mixer until smooth and well blended. Add the sour cream and vanilla extract, mixing until fully incorporated.
  4. Add the eggs: Beat in the eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed just until combined—avoid overmixing.
  5. Assemble and bake: Pour the cheesecake mixture over the prepared crust. Bake in the preheated oven for 1 hour to 1 hour 10 minutes, or until the center is almost set.
  6. Cool and refrigerate: Run a knife around the rim of the pan to loosen the cheesecake. Let it cool completely before removing the pan’s rim. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours to set.
  7. Serve: Top with cherry pie filling before serving. Enjoy!
